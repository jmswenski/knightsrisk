Core Articles

Workspace Onboarding

Start by creating an organization workspace, completing the profile, and setting the primary region. The region helps support regional sharing defaults and organization discovery.

  • Create the organization account from the signup page.
  • Complete onboarding before inviting the rest of the team.
  • Activate billing when the workspace is ready for live operational use.

Incident Submission

Reports should be operationally useful and privacy-aware. Capture the event time, severity, location, visibility scope, tags, summary, and narrative details.

  • Use saved organization sites when available for cleaner map placement.
  • Prefer directly observed facts over assumptions.
  • Use selected-partner visibility only when a trusted partner audience is intended.

Review Queue

Reviewers move reports through explicit moderation states. Use claim, request changes, verify, reject, and return-to-review actions deliberately.

  • Add reviewer notes when context affects the decision.
  • Use request changes when the report needs reporter clarification.
  • Verify only when the report is ready for its selected audience.

Trusted Partners

Trusted organization relationships should be explicit. Castlewatch tracks requests, connection state, and template MOU/NDA status for partner coordination.

  • Send partner requests only to organizations your team intends to coordinate with.
  • Track signing metadata for the template MOU and NDA before relying on a relationship operationally.
  • Review partner visibility defaults after new connections are established.

Organization Settings

Organization administrators can manage member roles, notification defaults, mapped sites, policy thresholds, sharing defaults, and profile details.

  • Keep membership roles least-privilege.
  • Maintain mapped sites for accurate dashboard placement.
  • Set notification defaults before broadening reporting access.

Common Troubleshooting

If a workflow appears blocked, check account status, organization status, billing state, and role permissions first.

  • Onboarding loops usually mean the organization profile has not been activated.
  • Reporting and review pages require an active subscription state.
  • Admin-only settings depend on the policy thresholds configured by the organization.
